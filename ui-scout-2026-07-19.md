## IZANAMI — https://izanami-official.com/  (Awwwards SOTD, Jul 19, 2026 — baqemono.inc / Tomoyuki Nakata)

WHAT IT DOES: A single-scroll narrative site built on a two-color palette (near-black `#0A0801` on off-white `#D9D7D4`) that stitches together GSAP ScrollSmoother-driven section pinning, a full-viewport WebGL fluid/ink simulation layer under the type, and a section-to-section masked transition where the next chapter's typography enters through an SVG clip-path that follows the scroll velocity. Layout is a strict 12-column grid with oversized editorial display type (PP-style neue-grotesk), and horizontal-scrolling parallax slabs interrupt the vertical flow at the "School / Craft / Retreat" pillars. Navigation is a magnetic-cursor sticky header that collapses to an icon after the hero, plus a bottom-right progress capsule.

PACKAGES:
- `next@15` (App Router) or `astro@5` (same pattern works either way — IZANAMI ships as SSG)
- `gsap@3.13`, `gsap/ScrollTrigger`, `gsap/ScrollSmoother`, `gsap/SplitText`, `gsap/DrawSVGPlugin` (Club GSAP — now fully free as of GSAP 3.13)
- `three@0.171` for the fluid sim; `@react-three/fiber@9`, `@react-three/drei@10`, `@react-three/postprocessing@3` if you go React
- `lenis@1.3` as a fallback for iOS momentum (ScrollSmoother handles desktop)
- `simplex-noise@4` for the ink displacement field
- `motion@12` (Framer Motion successor package) for micro-interactions only
- Fonts: `@fontsource-variable/inter-tight` or self-hosted PP Neue Montreal; Noto Serif JP for the kanji accents
- `clsx`, `tailwindcss@4`, `@tailwindcss/postcss`

IMPLEMENTATION:
1. Root layout: fixed WebGL canvas at `position: fixed; inset: 0; z-index: -1; pointer-events: none`. Content lives in `<main data-scroll-container>` with `overflow: hidden` on `<html>` and ScrollSmoother `smooth: 1.4, effects: true`.
2. Hero: `<section>` at 100dvh, kanji title split with `SplitText` into chars, staggered `y: 40 → 0, opacity: 0 → 1, duration: 0.9, ease: "expo.out", stagger: 0.03`. English subtitle fades in `delay: 0.6`.
3. Fluid canvas: Three.js `WebGLRenderTarget` ping-pong at 0.5× DPR, advection shader driven by pointer velocity + `simplex-noise` scroll offset. Blend with page via `mix-blend-mode: multiply` on a full-viewport `<div>` over the canvas.
4. Pillar sections: pin each with `ScrollTrigger.create({ trigger, start: "top top", end: "+=100%", pin: true, scrub: 1 })` and drive a horizontal `xPercent: -100` on the inner track.
5. Chapter transitions: SVG `<clipPath>` with a wavy `d` attribute animated by `DrawSVGPlugin`; the next section's background wipes over the previous using `clip-path: url(#chapter-wipe)`, `duration: 1.2, ease: "power3.inOut"`.
6. Magnetic cursor: 12px dot follows pointer with `lerp 0.15`; on `data-magnetic` hover it snaps to element center with `gsap.to(cursor, { x, y, duration: 0.35, ease: "power4.out" })`.
7. Breakpoints: `sm 640 / md 768 / lg 1024 / xl 1280 / 2xl 1536`. Disable ScrollSmoother + horizontal pins below `lg`; fall back to native scroll and stacked sections.
8. Perf: `ScrollTrigger.config({ ignoreMobileResize: true })`, `renderer.setPixelRatio(Math.min(devicePixelRatio, 1.5))`, `frameloop="demand"` for R3F, `content-visibility: auto` on off-screen sections.

WHY IT WORKS: The two-color palette forces every hierarchy decision onto typography, scale, and negative space — Gestalt figure-ground does the heavy lifting instead of color. The fluid layer is subordinate (multiply blend, low DPR) so it reads as texture, not spectacle — classic "motion in service of narrative" per Rams #4. Scroll-pinned pillars turn a marketing page into a paced document, which is the difference between a site users scan and one they finish.

GOOD / BAD: Best-in-class typographic hierarchy and disciplined restraint — nothing on screen is decorative. Bad: mobile falls back so aggressively that the WebGL narrative disappears entirely, and there is zero prefers-reduced-motion handling on the pinned pillars.

CLEAN APPLICATION: Apply this to Clean's landing-page hero + "How Clean thinks" narrative section (tryclean.ai/#how) by using a two-color palette (Clean off-white on near-black), replacing the fluid ink with a subtle animated dot-grid noise field, and pinning three scroll-scrubbed pillars — "Find," "Enrich," "Route" — that horizontally reveal the three sub-agents of the Growth Scaler. Keep the WebGL layer at 30% opacity and `mix-blend-mode: screen` so it reads as infrastructure texture, not marketing polish.

## Hiroto Sato Portfolio — https://hirotos.com/  (Awwwards SOTD + Developer Award, Jul 17, 2026)

WHAT IT DOES: Solo creative-developer portfolio built as a single R3F scene with routed sub-scenes. Project cards are 3D planes arranged on a spline path; scrolling advances a camera along the curve, and each project's plane rotates to face camera on approach. Post-processing chromatic aberration and film grain kick in during scroll velocity spikes. UI overlay is HTML in a `<Html transform>` block, so DOM text sits inside the 3D world at correct depth. Hover on a card triggers a Blender-baked mesh morph and pulls audio-reactive frequency data from a WebAudio ambient loop.

PACKAGES:
- `three@0.171`, `@react-three/fiber@9`, `@react-three/drei@10` (`Html`, `useCurve`, `useScroll`, `MeshTransmissionMaterial`), `@react-three/postprocessing@3`, `@react-three/rapier@2` (for the collision hit-tests on 3D nav)
- `gsap@3.13` for timeline choreography of the camera + `gsap/ScrollTrigger`
- `three-mesh-bvh` for accelerated raycasts against baked meshes
- `leva` in dev, stripped in prod
- `next@15` + `react@19`
- `tailwindcss@4`, `radix-ui/react-tooltip`
- `tone@15` for the ambient audio graph

IMPLEMENTATION:
1. `<Canvas dpr={[1, 1.75]} gl={{ antialias: false, powerPreference: "high-performance" }} frameloop="demand">`; enable demand loop only during scroll/hover via `invalidate()` inside a scroll listener.
2. Curve from `THREE.CatmullRomCurve3` with 8 control points; `useScroll()` from drei drives `curve.getPointAt(scroll.offset)` for the camera position and `curve.getTangentAt` for `lookAt`.
3. Project planes instanced via `InstancedMesh`; per-instance uniforms set through `onBeforeCompile` to inject a per-card scroll-progress uniform driving hover reveal.
4. `MeshTransmissionMaterial` on the hero glass slab (`thickness: 0.5, roughness: 0.05, chromaticAberration: 0.06, distortion: 0.3`).
5. Postprocessing pipeline: `EffectComposer` → `ChromaticAberration` (offset lerps 0.001 → 0.008 with scroll velocity) → `Noise` (opacity 0.06) → `Vignette` (offset 0.3, darkness 0.5).
6. HTML overlays: `<Html transform occlude portal={{ current: gl.domElement.parentElement }} distanceFactor={10}>` for each project's title so DOM handles crawlability and 3D handles the vibe.
7. Motion budget: on mobile (`matchMedia("(pointer: coarse)")`) collapse to a 2D scroll list of the same cards; keep the audio loop optional behind a first-tap unlock.
8. `prefers-reduced-motion`: freeze camera on scroll, cross-fade instead of morph.

WHY IT WORKS: The 3D scene isn't decoration — it *is* the navigation. Camera-along-a-curve gives inevitability (Hick's law drops toward zero, there is one path), and per-card focal-plane rotation applies the Gestalt law of common fate. Chromatic aberration on velocity is a rare example of motion feedback that encodes physical inertia without being cutesy.

GOOD / BAD: Best-in-class: DOM-in-3D done correctly (accessible + performant + occlusion-aware). Bad: initial load is 4.2 MB compressed, first-input-delay on a mid-tier Android is over 400ms — the ambient audio graph auto-loads even when muted.

CLEAN APPLICATION: Apply this to Clean's product demo section (tryclean.ai/#demo) by replacing the traditional autoplay-loop with a scroll-along-a-spline camera that flies through *three floating dashboard "moments"* — a Discover query, an Enrich pass, and a CRM write — each rendered as an `<Html transform>` panel of the real product UI inside the 3D scene. The camera advances on scroll; users watch the workflow assemble itself in a way a video can't match, and a real, indexable DOM lives inside it for SEO.

## Aceternity UI — Background Ripple / Encrypted Text / Layout Text Flip Suite — https://ui.aceternity.com/components  (rolling drops through July 2026)

WHAT IT DOES: A copy-paste React component library (Tailwind v4 + Motion for React) that ships four newly-added text and background components perfect for AI/dev-tool marketing pages: Background Ripple Effect (canvas-driven concentric pulses on click/hover), Encrypted Text (character scramble that resolves to final string on view), Layout Text Flip (rotating word inside a fluid-width capsule that animates its border), and Sparkles (particle field that reacts to cursor). All are self-contained, unstyled beyond Tailwind classes, and expose motion configs through props.

PACKAGES:
- `next@15`, `react@19`
- `tailwindcss@4`, `tailwind-merge`, `clsx`, `class-variance-authority`
- `motion@12` (the successor to `framer-motion` — same API, smaller bundle)
- `@radix-ui/react-slot`
- `lucide-react` for icons
- Optional: `canvas-confetti@1.9` for the sparkles substrate
- No 3D dependency for this cluster — everything is 2D canvas or DOM/SVG

IMPLEMENTATION:
1. Background Ripple: `<canvas>` sized to parent via `ResizeObserver`; on pointer event, push `{x, y, t0, maxR}` into a ref array. On each `requestAnimationFrame`, iterate and draw circles with `ctx.strokeStyle = 'rgba(255,255,255,' + (1 - progress) + ')'` where `progress = (now - t0) / duration (1200ms)`. Prune when progress > 1. Blend by placing canvas behind hero and using `mix-blend-mode: overlay`.
2. Encrypted Text: `useInView` (motion) to trigger; `useEffect` runs a 40ms interval that fills each char slot with a random ASCII glyph from a `!@#$%^&*<>?/{}[]` pool, then progressively locks characters left-to-right on a curve `easeOutCubic`. Total duration `chars * 30ms + 400ms`.
3. Layout Text Flip: `<motion.span layout>` inside a `<motion.div layout transition={{ layout: { type: 'spring', stiffness: 220, damping: 22 } }}>` with an `AnimatePresence mode="popLayout"` cycling children on a 2.4s interval. The parent capsule border uses `background-clip: padding-box, border-box` with a conic-gradient border that rotates via `@property --angle` + `animation: spin 6s linear infinite`.
4. Sparkles: `<canvas>`; spawn 60 particles with `{x, y, vx, vy, life}`, on pointer proximity boost `vx/vy` outward, decay life at 1/60s per frame, respawn on death. Render as 1–2px squares with `globalCompositeOperation = 'lighter'`.
5. Tokens: define `--brand-fg`, `--brand-bg`, `--brand-accent` at `:root` and reference from every component so a single theme swap re-skins all four.
6. All four honor `prefers-reduced-motion`: bypass canvas loops and render the resolved final state.

WHY IT WORKS: Each component isolates *one* affordance and executes it at high polish — this is the "small components, unbundled" doctrine that made shadcn/ui win. Encrypted Text triggers pattern-completion satisfaction (Gestalt closure). Ripple provides direct-manipulation feedback that maps 1:1 with user intent, satisfying Nielsen heuristic #1 (visibility of system status) at the marketing layer. Layout Text Flip uses shared-element transitions instead of fade, so the eye stays anchored — no attention re-acquisition cost.

GOOD / BAD: Best-in-class: copy-paste ergonomics + zero lock-in + Tailwind v4 tokens are exemplary. Bad: several components ship without SSR-safe hydration guards, and the sparkles canvas doesn't `pause on document.hidden` — a laptop lid on a background tab still burns cycles.

CLEAN APPLICATION: Apply this to Clean's landing-page hero H1 by using Layout Text Flip to rotate "Clean finds [buyers / partners / hires / champions] you didn't know existed" on a 2.8s beat, and pair it with a subtle Background Ripple layer that only fires on the "Get started" CTA hover — makes the hero feel alive without a single third-party embed or video.

## shadcn/ui + shadcnblocks — Analytics Bento Dashboard Blocks (July 14, 2026 drop, 396 new blocks) — https://www.shadcnblocks.com/new

WHAT IT DOES: A collection of copy-paste dashboard blocks in shadcn's registry-JSON format that compose full analytics surfaces from primitive `Card`, `Chart`, `Sidebar`, and `Sheet` pieces. The July 14 drop leans into "isolated bento" — 3-to-4 column asymmetric grids where each tile hosts a small-multiple chart, KPI stat, or activity feed, all with scroll-triggered `motion` entrance staggers. Charts are Recharts underneath, styled through CSS variables (`--chart-1` … `--chart-5`), and the sidebar is the shadcn `Sidebar` primitive with collapsible groups and command-palette (`cmd+k`) search.

PACKAGES:
- `shadcn@2` CLI (`npx shadcn@latest add https://shadcnblocks.com/r/<block>.json`)
- `next@15`, `react@19`, `typescript@5.6`
- `tailwindcss@4`, `@tailwindcss/postcss`, `tailwindcss-animate`, `tw-animate-css`
- `@radix-ui/react-*` (dialog, dropdown-menu, popover, tooltip, tabs, scroll-area)
- `recharts@2.15` (or `recharts@3` if you're on the beta)
- `lucide-react`, `sonner`, `cmdk`
- `motion@12` for the entrance staggers
- `date-fns@4`, `zod@3`, `@tanstack/react-table@8` for data tables

IMPLEMENTATION:
1. Grid: `grid grid-cols-1 md:grid-cols-6 gap-4` with per-tile `md:col-span-{2,3,4,6}` classes; tallest bento cell is `row-span-2`.
2. Entrance: wrap each tile in `<motion.div initial={{ opacity: 0, y: 12 }} whileInView={{ opacity: 1, y: 0 }} viewport={{ once: true, margin: "-80px" }} transition={{ duration: 0.5, ease: [0.22, 1, 0.36, 1], delay: index * 0.05 }}>`.
3. KPI tile: `Card` with `CardHeader` → `CardDescription` (label) → `CardTitle` at `text-3xl font-semibold tabular-nums` → footer `Badge` with trend arrow (green up, red down) using `lucide-react` `TrendingUp/Down`.
4. Chart tile: `<ChartContainer config={chartConfig}>` around a Recharts `<AreaChart>` with `linearGradient` fill, `stroke="var(--chart-1)"`, `strokeWidth={2}`, `type="monotone"`, `dot={false}`, and a custom `ChartTooltip` from shadcn.
5. Sidebar: `<SidebarProvider>` → `<Sidebar collapsible="icon">`; groups `Overview / Prospects / Runs / Settings`; footer holds user menu.
6. Command palette: `<CommandDialog>` bound to `cmd+k` via a `useHotkeys` hook; sections `Actions / Recent / Docs`.
7. Theming: single `globals.css` with `oklch()` tokens (shadcn moved off HSL in v2) — swap `--primary`, `--chart-1..5` to re-skin.
8. Perf: dynamic-import chart tiles with `next/dynamic({ ssr: false })` to keep the shell TTFB clean; skeletons for each tile mirror final dimensions to prevent CLS.

WHY IT WORKS: Bento grids solve the Von Restorff problem for dashboards — asymmetry makes the KPI tile pop without color contrast alone. Scroll-triggered entrance staggers create perceived responsiveness (Fitts's law analogue: motion draws the pointer to actionable regions). Copy-paste + registry pattern means the design system is versioned in git, not locked behind a Figma library — this is why every serious 2026 dev tool ships on shadcn.

GOOD / BAD: Best-in-class: the shadcn registry-JSON pattern with `npx shadcn add <url>` is now the de-facto distribution channel for design systems. Bad: many community blocks skip `aria-*` on custom-styled buttons and don't tab-trap inside their dialogs.

CLEAN APPLICATION: Apply this to Clean's in-product dashboard (app.tryclean.ai/dashboard) by rebuilding the "Run overview" screen as a 6-column bento with (a) a hero KPI tile `Prospects surfaced this week`, (b) a wide `AreaChart` of enrichment throughput, (c) a `Sparkline` tile for CRM writes, (d) a tall `Activity` feed showing agent turns, and (e) a `Command` palette bound to `⌘K` for jumping to any lead or run. Ship it via the shadcn registry so Pratham and Tejas can pull tile updates with a single CLI command.

## Codrops — Scroll-Revealed WebGL Gallery (GSAP + Three.js + Astro + Barba.js) — https://tympanus.net/codrops/2026/02/02/building-a-scroll-revealed-webgl-gallery-with-gsap-three-js-astro-and-barba-js/

WHAT IT DOES: A multi-page image gallery where each thumbnail is a Three.js plane with a custom fragment shader that reveals the image through a scroll-driven mask (noise-displaced circular reveal). Page navigation uses Barba.js for SPA-style transitions: outgoing page shrinks and blurs, incoming page enters with a WebGL displacement swipe. Smooth scroll via GSAP ScrollSmoother; each plane's shader receives a `uProgress` uniform tied to its viewport position.

PACKAGES:
- `astro@5` (SSG multi-page)
- `three@0.171`
- `gsap@3.13`, `gsap/ScrollTrigger`, `gsap/ScrollSmoother`
- `@barba/core@2.10`, `@barba/prefetch@2.2`
- `glslify` or Astro's `?raw` shader loader
- `imagesloaded@5` (or a `Promise.all(img.decode())` shim)
- Static asset pipeline via `astro:assets` with `format: "avif"` and `widths: [400, 800, 1600]`
- No React — plain TS

IMPLEMENTATION:
1. Boot: single Three.js `WebGLRenderer` attached to a `position: fixed` canvas; one `Scene`, one orthographic-ish `PerspectiveCamera` (fov 25, distance 10).
2. Per-thumbnail: read DOM rect via `getBoundingClientRect`, map to world coordinates using `(x - vw/2) / vw * frustumWidth`, keep a `sync()` fn that runs each frame to reposition planes to match DOM.
3. Fragment shader: sample image texture, sample noise texture, compute mask `step(uProgress + noise * 0.1, distance(uv, vec2(0.5)))`, `mix(bgColor, texColor, 1 - mask)`.
4. Scroll: ScrollSmoother wraps content; `ScrollTrigger.create({ trigger: '.thumb', start: 'top bottom', end: 'top 20%', onUpdate: self => plane.material.uniforms.uProgress.value = self.progress })`.
5. Barba: `barba.init({ transitions: [{ leave: async ({ current }) => gsap.to(current.container, { scale: 0.9, opacity: 0, filter: 'blur(10px)', duration: 0.6, ease: 'expo.inOut' }), enter: ... }] })`. Prefetch on hover with `@barba/prefetch`.
6. Prevent FOUC: `astro:page-load` waits for `imagesLoaded` before revealing.
7. Perf: cap DPR at 1.5, share a single `PlaneGeometry` across all thumbs, texture atlas the noise map, dispose textures on `barba:before-leave`.

WHY IT WORKS: The scroll-revealed mask ties motion to a physical metaphor (peeking through fog), which respects the principle of natural mapping. Barba transitions preserve object continuity across page loads, avoiding the "start over" feeling of a hard nav — this alone raises perceived quality more than any single visual effect. Uniform-driven reveals mean animation is bounded by a *state*, not a *timeline*, so scrubbing back always looks correct.

GOOD / BAD: Best-in-class: DOM-and-WebGL sync approach is textbook and portable to any framework. Bad: Barba + Astro's view transitions are now overlapping features in 2026 — reaching for Barba is a legacy call, and Astro's native `<ClientRouter />` accomplishes 80% of it without a fifth dependency.

CLEAN APPLICATION: Apply this to Clean's `/customers` case-study index page by rendering each customer logo as a WebGL plane with a scroll-driven color-shift reveal (mono → brand color as the plane enters the viewport), and use Astro's built-in `<ClientRouter />` (not Barba) for the case-study detail page transitions — the outgoing card scales and blurs, the detail page's hero image enters with the same displacement swipe. Ties the marketing surface to a single visual grammar without shipping React.

KEY CONSTRAINTS: Clean is developer infrastructure — the result must feel technically sophisticated and fast, not flashy or generic. Specifically:
- **Do not** ship a full-page WebGL scene. WebGL is limited to (a) the ambient hero noise field at low DPR, and (b) the three product-UI panels in the pinned narrative. Nothing else.
- **Do not** use gradient meshes, glassmorphism, floating orbs, or any "AI-startup default" hero background — those all read as generic in July 2026.
- **Do not** autoplay video anywhere; the pinned narrative *is* the demo.
- **Do not** ship without `prefers-reduced-motion` fallback for every animated primitive.
- **Do not** exceed 180 KB gzipped JS on first paint; dynamic-import Three.js and R3F behind a `whileInView` boundary so the hero paints from HTML alone.
- **Do** keep the palette to two colors plus one accent used only on the CTA and one status indicator.
- **Do** use Geist Mono for section eyebrows and pillar labels — matches the Vercel/Linear visual grammar developers already trust.
- **Do** ensure the hero H1, all pillar copy, and product-panel screenshots are real DOM (crawlable, translatable, selectable).
