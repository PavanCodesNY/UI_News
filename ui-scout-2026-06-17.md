## 1. Fauna Robotics â€” https://faunarobotics.com/ (Awwwards SOTD 2026-06-17, Developer Award)

WHAT IT DOES: A scroll-pinned single-page narrative whose viewport is hijacked by a full-bleed Three.js scene of "Sprout," a humanoid robot. The scene is GLTF-loaded once, then a ScrollTrigger timeline drives camera position, FOV, model bone rotations, and a swap of HDRI environments through five chapters (Coexist, Capable, Safe, Fun, Build). Text blocks fade in/out absolutely positioned over the canvas while the camera dollies â€” there is no traditional "section change," just a continuous orbit. The model exposes interactive hotspots that the cursor hovers reveal as tooltip cards anchored in 3D space (sprite billboards with HTML overlay via drei `Html` semantic).

PACKAGES:
- `next` (14, app router)
- `three` (^0.160)
- `@react-three/fiber`, `@react-three/drei`, `@react-three/postprocessing`
- `gsap` + `@gsap/react` (ScrollTrigger + Observer plugins; club license)
- `lenis` (smooth scroll proxy)
- `tailwindcss` + `clsx` + `tailwind-merge`
- `framer-motion` (DOM-layer text reveals)
- `geist` font (Geist Sans + Mono via `next/font`)
- `lucide-react` (chrome icons)

IMPLEMENTATION:
1. Component tree: `<Lenis>` provider wraps `<ScrollScene>` (a pinned 100vh `<Canvas dpr={[1,2]} gl={{antialias:true, powerPreference:'high-performance'}}>`) and `<NarrativeOverlay>` (absolute-positioned `<section>`s, each `100vh`, totaling 500vh).
2. Inside `Canvas`: `<PerspectiveCamera makeDefault fov={28} near={0.1} far={50}>`, `<Environment preset="studio">` swapped per chapter using a Zustand-stored `chapterIndex`, `<Sprout>` (GLTFJSX-generated component), `<EffectComposer><Bloom intensity={0.35} luminanceThreshold={0.85}/><Vignette eskil={false} offset={0.2} darkness={0.6}/></EffectComposer>`.
3. Scroll driver: register `gsap.registerPlugin(ScrollTrigger)`; one master timeline scrubbed `scrub: 1.2`, `pin: true`, `end: '+=5000'`. Each chapter is a `.to()` on a `proxy` object holding `cam.x, cam.y, cam.z, cam.target, jointL.rot.y, hdriBlend`. In `useFrame`, lerp the actual camera + model from the proxy with `damp(current, target, 6, delta)` from `maath/easing`.
4. Tooltip hotspots: `<group position={[x,y,z]} userData={{label}}><Html center distanceFactor={8} occlude="blending"><HotCard/></Html></group>`. Tooltip card uses `backdrop-blur-md bg-white/6 border border-white/12 rounded-xl px-3 py-2`.
5. Typography: oversized `text-[clamp(56px,9vw,180px)]` Geist Sans, `font-weight: 500`, `tracking-[-0.04em]`, `leading-[0.88]`. Body `text-[15px] leading-[1.55] text-white/65`.
6. Color tokens: `bg #0A0A0B`, `fg #F4F4F5`, accent `#A3E635` (limey green) used only for the Sprout chest LED and the CTA.
7. Easing canon: `power3.out` for chapter punches, `expo.inOut` for camera dollies, `back.out(1.4)` for hotspot reveals. Durations 1.1s / 1.8s / 0.6s respectively.
8. Performance: `dpr={[1, Math.min(2, window.devicePixelRatio)]}`, draco-compressed GLB, `<Bvh>` for hit-testing hotspots, `useGLTF.preload('/sprout.glb')` at module scope.
9. Mobile: replace pinned canvas with a 60fps WebM looped video poster (no scroll-scrub on mobile) at <768px and disable postprocessing; chapter overlays stack vertically.

WHY IT WORKS: Single-protagonist focal point (Gestalt figure/ground) anchored against neutral void â€” every pixel works for the robot. Motion semantics: the camera moves in service of a sentence, never the other way around, so each scroll tick "answers" the previous text. Whitespace rhythm in the typography stack lets the eye breathe between heavy 3D frames.

GOOD: Best-in-class integration of scroll-narrative + 3D character â€” no orphaned "wow" moments, every frame serves the story.
BAD: 4MB+ GLB + HDRIs means a 2.2s LCP on cold cache; mobile fallback is graceful but loses the entire thesis of the page.

CLEAN APPLICATION: Apply this to Clean's landing page hero by replacing Sprout with a stylized 3D "agent swarm" â€” 100 low-poly soft-cube agents floating in a loose cluster, each emitting a glowing line to a central "context nucleus." Scroll chapter 1: "100 agents per person." Cam orbits cluster. Chapter 2: "None of them share context." Lines go gray, cluster disperses. Chapter 3: "Clean gives them one brain." Lines reconnect to nucleus, cluster snaps into orbital rings. Chapter 4: "4-5x fewer tokens, half the task time." Numbers fly in as 3D billboards. Use the exact GSAP+R3F+Lenis stack above; bake the GLB as a single 800KB asset; ship mobile fallback as a 6-second looped MP4. This is the only hero Clean should ship in Q3.

## 2. Elva â€” https://elva-labs.com/ (Awwwards SOTD 2026-06-16, Webby for Best Visual UI in AI)

WHAT IT DOES: A WebGL "glass blob" persona sits centered on the page and morphs through 30+ named behavioral states (idle, listening, thinking, excited, error, etc.) driven by either scroll position or click triggers. The blob is a marching-cubes metaball mesh with a refractive shader (IOR ~1.45, dispersion, frosted internal noise) lit by a 3-light rig and rendered against a soft gradient backdrop. Surrounding the blob: minimal mobile-frame mockups slide in/out of view in response to which blob state is active, simulating a live conversation between the user and the AI.

PACKAGES:
- `next`, `react`, `react-dom`
- `three`, `@react-three/fiber`, `@react-three/drei` (`MeshTransmissionMaterial`, `Environment`, `useScroll`)
- `leva` (dev-only controls during tuning)
- `gsap` (ScrollTrigger for state stepping)
- `framer-motion` (mobile-frame mockup transitions)
- `lenis`
- `tailwindcss`
- `inter` + `instrument-serif` (display)
- A custom GLSL noise pack (`glsl-noise` via `glslify-loader`) for the internal frosted layer

IMPLEMENTATION:
1. Blob core: `<mesh><icosahedronGeometry args={[1.4, 64]} /><MeshTransmissionMaterial transmission={1} roughness={0.05} thickness={1.2} ior={1.45} chromaticAberration={0.05} backside resolution={1024} samples={6} anisotropy={0.3} /></mesh>`. Subdivide further only on desktop.
2. State machine: a `useReducer` with the 30 states; each state contains `{ scale, rotation, noiseAmp, noiseFreq, colorA, colorB, eyeOffset }`. On state change, `gsap.to(state, {... duration: 0.6, ease: 'expo.out'})` and read in `useFrame` to mutate uniforms via refs (never setState in frame).
3. Vertex displacement: a custom `onBeforeCompile` patch injects `position += normal * snoise(position * uFreq + uTime*0.3) * uAmp;` so the blob actually shape-shifts. `uAmp` ranges 0.02 (idle) to 0.22 (excited).
4. Eyes: two small `<sphereGeometry>` children parented to the blob, offset by `eyeOffset` per state â€” this is how it conveys emotion without a face.
5. Backdrop: a single full-bleed `<div>` with `background: radial-gradient(60% 50% at 50% 40%, #FFF5E1 0%, #F5E7CB 40%, #E9D6A8 100%);` â€” warm beige, not black. The blob's chromatic aberration sells against warm tones.
6. Scroll-locked storytelling: ScrollTrigger snaps to 7 sections; each section calls `setState('listening' | 'thinking' | ...)`. Between sections, mobile-frame mockups (`framer-motion` `<motion.div>` with `layoutId`) shared-element-animate from one position to another.
7. Type: H1 `Instrument Serif 96/0.9 -0.02em` for emotional moments, `Inter 18/1.5` for body. Mixing serif display + neutral sans for body is the entire identity.
8. Performance: cap blob resolution to 512 on retina mobile, drop transmission samples to 3, freeze noise updates when blob is offscreen (`useFrame` early-return on `inView` IntersectionObserver).

WHY IT WORKS: A single anthropomorphized object replaces 30 stock UI screenshots â€” the blob IS the product personality, not a decoration around it. Warm gradient + glass refraction creates a Vermeer-like soft luminance that competes with no other element on page (Gestalt isolation). Each behavioral state maps one-to-one to a copy line, so motion teaches function.

GOOD: The blob's 30-state library doubles as the in-app loading/processing UI â€” landing page and product share the same visual primitive.
BAD: `MeshTransmissionMaterial` at 1024 resolution + 6 samples is a 35ms frame on a M1 MacBook Air; on mid-range Android the blob frame-skips and looks aliased.

CLEAN APPLICATION: Apply this to Clean's product demo section (just below the hero) by building a single WebGL "context orb" that lives in the right column while the left column scroll-narrates the agent's workflow. States: `quiet` (orb pulses slowly) â†’ `query` (orb scatters into 100 micro-particles labeled with agent names) â†’ `retrieve` (particles fly into context sources: GitHub, Linear, Slack) â†’ `unified` (particles return as one cohesive sphere). Each state maps to a Clean value prop. Use the exact transmission-material + custom-noise vertex displacement above, but keep the orb monochrome (Clean lime `#A3E635` against deep `#0A0A0B`) â€” don't import Elva's warm palette wholesale, that's their voice not ours.

## 3. Sakazuki â€” https://www.awwwards.com/sites/sakazuki (Awwwards SOTD 2026-06-14, Typography Honors May 2026)

WHAT IT DOES: A two-color (#C30D23 red, #E1D6CE bone) typographic-led site for a Japanese food/drink concept. Type is set in a tall display serif at 32â€“55vw and intentionally clipped by viewport edges, while photographic plates (sakazuki cups, food) slide horizontally on scroll using a SplitText character-by-character reveal synced to a horizontal `<section>` translate. Cursor is a custom red dot that ink-bleeds into a circle on hover over interactive elements. No 3D â€” all magic is type + image transform + cursor.

PACKAGES:
- `astro` (multi-page with view transitions) OR `nuxt` (the studio's preferred stack)
- `gsap` + `SplitText` + `ScrollTrigger` + `Flip` (club license required for SplitText)
- `lenis` (smooth scroll proxy)
- `barba.js` (page transitions if not Astro)
- `tailwindcss` + `@tailwindcss/typography`
- Self-hosted display serif (`Editorial New` or `Reckless Neue`) + a Japanese system fallback (`'Noto Serif JP'`)
- Sharp/Next image for AVIF crops

IMPLEMENTATION:
1. Layout: 8-column CSS grid, `grid-template-columns: repeat(8, 1fr); gap: 24px;`. Display type uses `grid-column: 1 / -1; font-size: clamp(180px, 38vw, 720px); line-height: 0.78; letter-spacing: -0.045em;`.
2. SplitText reveal: `SplitText.create('.hero-h1', {type: 'chars,words'})`. Each char wrapped in a `<span class="char">`. Initial state `yPercent: 120, rotate: 12deg, opacity: 0`. On scroll trigger: `gsap.to('.char', {yPercent:0, rotate:0, opacity:1, stagger: 0.018, ease: 'expo.out', duration: 1.1})`.
3. Horizontal section: a single 100vh container, inside it a flex row `width: 400vw` that translates `x: -300vw` over a 4000px scrubbed ScrollTrigger. Photo plates inside the row have their own parallax: `gsap.to('.plate img', {xPercent: 30, ease: 'none', scrollTrigger: {trigger: '.plate', scrub: true, horizontal: true}})`.
4. Custom cursor: `<div id="cursor"/>` fixed, position updated in a single `gsap.quickSetter('#cursor', 'css')` call in a `requestAnimationFrame` loop. On `mouseenter` of `[data-cursor="link"]`, scale 1 â†’ 4 with `gsap.to({scale, mixBlendMode:'difference'}, {duration: 0.4, ease: 'power3.out'})`.
5. Color: only two HEX values exist in the entire build. No tints, no shades. Discipline.
6. Page transitions: `astro:transitions` `slide` for full-page wipes, paired with `view-transition-name` on the hero image so the image flies between pages.
7. Performance: serve all images as AVIF + a WebP fallback, `loading="lazy"` everywhere except hero, preload the display serif WOFF2, `font-display: block` (because clipped FOUT would break the layout).
8. Mobile: horizontal scroll section becomes vertical stack with `flex-direction: column`; type clamps down to `clamp(72px, 16vw, 140px)`.

WHY IT WORKS: Two-color rule forces all hierarchy into typography and scale (Tufte / MĂĽller-Brockmann discipline). Clipping huge type by viewport teaches the eye that there's more to discover â€” the page rewards scroll without using arrows or hints. Cursor-as-ink-dot maps brand to interaction; even idle states are on-brand.

GOOD: Pure typographic restraint with one wow moment (the horizontal section) â€” every other site this week is showing off; this one knows when to stop.
BAD: `mix-blend-mode: difference` on the cursor breaks contrast on photographic backgrounds and fails WCAG AA for focus visibility.

CLEAN APPLICATION: Apply this to Clean's docs navigation by stripping the docs site to a two-color palette (`#0A0A0B` ink, `#F4F4F5` page) with a single accent for code (`#A3E635`). Use SplitText to animate section titles on route change, treating every doc page like a magazine spread. The cursor-as-ink-dot becomes a cursor-as-context-cursor: a small `~` glyph that grows to a square on hover over code blocks and to a play triangle over runnable examples. The horizontal-scroll section pattern is perfect for the "Agents Ă— Context Sources" matrix page â€” each agent slides across as you scroll, showing what context it consumes.

## 4. Tresmares Capital â€” https://www.awwwards.com/sites/tresmares-capital (Awwwards SOTD 2026-06-12, Developer Award, by Dgrees)

WHAT IT DOES: A single-color (#D81413 red on white) corporate site for an investment firm. The visual concept is "Pico Tres" â€” a triangular peak â€” rendered entirely as animated SVG paths that fold, unfold, and morph across sections via GSAP's `MorphSVG` plugin. Parallax depth comes from three SVG layers translating at different rates against a fixed background. Scroll cadence is metronomic: every section snaps to a position with `scroll-snap-align: start` enforced by Lenis programmatic scroll.

PACKAGES:
- `vite` + vanilla TS (no framework â€” Dgrees ships this way)
- `gsap` + `ScrollTrigger` + `MorphSVGPlugin` + `DrawSVGPlugin` (club license)
- `lenis`
- `tailwindcss` (utility-only, no component layer)
- `inter` + a geometric serif for marks
- SVGOMG-optimized SVGs

IMPLEMENTATION:
1. Architecture: 7 sections, each `min-height: 100vh`. A persistent fixed-position `<svg id="peak" viewBox="0 0 1440 900">` lives behind all content and contains three `<path>` layers for foreground / mid / back peak silhouettes.
2. MorphSVG: pre-author 7 path states (one per section). On ScrollTrigger enter, `gsap.to('#peak-front', {morphSVG: '#peak-state-2', duration: 1.0, ease: 'power2.inOut'})`. Layer translate via `transform: translate3d(0, -${scrollProgress*120}px, 0)` for back, `-60px` for mid, `-180px` for front â€” classic 3-2-1 parallax ratio.
3. DrawSVG accents: section headers have an underline path drawn in on entry â€” `gsap.from('.section-rule', {drawSVG: '0%', duration: 0.9, ease: 'power3.out'})`.
4. Scroll snap: Lenis with `lerp: 0.08, smoothWheel: true, syncTouch: true`. Snap to section starts via `ScrollTrigger.snap({snapTo: 'labels', duration: 0.6, ease: 'power2.out'})`.
5. Typography: Inter for everything, single weight (500) for body, weight 600 for H1/H2. Sizes: H1 `clamp(56px, 7vw, 144px)`, H2 `40px`, body `17/1.55`. Tracking on H1 `-0.035em`.
6. Color discipline: `#D81413` red only on the peak SVG, headline rules, and CTAs. Everything else `#1A1A1A` on `#FFFFFF`. No tints.
7. Performance: SVG files are <8KB each after SVGO; `MorphSVGPlugin` runs entirely on the main thread but at this size never costs >2ms/frame. Lenis raf coupled with `gsap.ticker` (`gsap.ticker.add(time => lenis.raf(time*1000))`).
8. Mobile: kill MorphSVG transitions, keep only DrawSVG header reveals; parallax disabled (just `position: sticky` peak).

WHY IT WORKS: One color enforces a single visual idea: the peak. Morphing SVG is a low-key flex that signals craft to investor visitors without being garish â€” it's exactly the tone an alternative-finance firm needs. Snap scroll cadence makes the site feel measured and confident, which is the brand promise.

GOOD: The cheapest possible "wow" â€” no WebGL, no 3D, no video. Sub-100KB total motion budget. Lighthouse score easily 95+.
BAD: MorphSVG plugin requires GSAP club license, so this exact pattern locks small teams out unless they own the license.

CLEAN APPLICATION: Apply this to Clean's pricing page by replacing the peak with an animated "context graph" â€” three SVG layers representing context sources, agent calls, and unified output. Each pricing tier (Free / Pro / Enterprise) morphs the graph to show density: more nodes, more edges, more activity. Use the exact GSAP MorphSVG + DrawSVG stack and the same 3-layer parallax ratios. Hold the palette to `#0A0A0B` ink, `#F4F4F5` page, `#A3E635` accent on edges. This is high-confidence "we know what we're doing" energy for an enterprise pricing page â€” anti-flashy, pro-precise.

## 5. The Never Ending Story (Codrops technique) â€” https://tympanus.net/codrops/2026/05/28/the-never-ending-story-building-a-seamless-infinite-scroll-experience-with-gsap-lenis/

WHAT IT DOES: A seamless infinite-scroll pattern: visually duplicated first section appended after the last, scroll position invisibly snapped back when the user reaches the clone, parallax depth across 3 layers, and snap-based section control. The illusion is so smooth there is no "reset" frame. Built in plain HTML/CSS/JS â€” framework-agnostic.

PACKAGES:
- `gsap` (free tier â€” no club required)
- `lenis`
- Vanilla TS or any framework
- `tailwindcss` optional
- That's it. Sub-50KB JS.

IMPLEMENTATION:
1. DOM: `<main id="track"><section>1</section><section>2</section>...<section>N</section><section data-clone>1</section></main>`. The last section is a CSS-mirrored clone of the first.
2. Lenis: `const lenis = new Lenis({ lerp: 0.075, smoothWheel: true, infinite: false });`. Hook `gsap.ticker.add(t => lenis.raf(t*1000))`.
3. Loop detection: in `lenis.on('scroll', ({scroll}) => { if (scroll >= cloneStart) { lenis.scrollTo(0, {immediate: true}); } if (scroll <= 0 && delta < 0) { lenis.scrollTo(cloneStart - viewport, {immediate: true}); } })`. The `immediate: true` is what makes the reset invisible.
4. Parallax: three layers per section with `data-speed="0.4 | 0.7 | 1.0"`. In a `ScrollTrigger` per layer, `gsap.to(layer, { yPercent: -50 * speed, ease: 'none', scrollTrigger: {trigger: section, scrub: true}})`.
5. Snap: ScrollTrigger `snap: {snapTo: 1/sections, duration: {min: 0.3, max: 0.7}, ease: 'power2.out'}`.
6. Visual reinforcement: a thin progress dot column on the right that rotates through indices as you loop â€” this reassures the user they didn't break the page.
7. Performance: `will-change: transform` only during active scroll (toggle via `lenis.on('scroll')` start/stop); `transform: translate3d(0, ${y}px, 0)` on every layer to keep them on the compositor.

WHY IT WORKS: The "no reset frame" trick exploits attention blink â€” humans can't distinguish a 16ms position snap during smooth scroll. Snap cadence prevents fatigue. Parallax depth gives the illusion of distance without 3D. Total motion budget is tiny so the site feels weightless.

GOOD: Production-grade infinite scroll with zero memory leak risk â€” sections aren't being added/removed, just cloned once at build.
BAD: Loses URL-state for deep linking to a specific section unless you wire `history.replaceState` per snap stop â€” most implementations skip this.

CLEAN APPLICATION: Apply this to Clean's "Use Cases" or "Customer logos" carousel section by looping six logo+quote panels infinitely as the user scrolls past. Each panel = one Clean customer (Series A YC startups, 80+ pilots). Use the exact Lenis + GSAP snap + 3-layer parallax pattern. Background layer: faint code grid. Mid layer: customer logo. Foreground: oversized quote. Hold motion budget under 50KB. This replaces the static "logos in a row" pattern every other infra startup ships.

## BUILD DIRECTIVE

TARGET: **Clean landing page hero + product demo section (tryclean.ai), as a single continuous scroll-narrative.**

APPROACH: Build a 5-chapter pinned ScrollTrigger sequence using the Fauna Robotics architecture (R3F + GSAP + Lenis) as the structural backbone, then borrow the Elva blob behavior-state model to drive the centerpiece visual. But the centerpiece is not a humanoid robot, it's a **Context Orb**: a transmissive-glass sphere that scatters into 100 labeled agent particles (Chapter 2), connects each particle to context sources (Chapter 3), and resolves back into a single denser orb (Chapter 4). Final chapter (Chapter 5) is the CTA, with a horizontal Tresmares-style SVG morph graph showing token reduction (331k â†’ 71k) as the user lands. Type is the Sakazuki / Editorial New discipline â€” oversized display serif headlines clamped by viewport, two-color base (`#0A0A0B` / `#F4F4F5`) with `#A3E635` as the only accent. Background section between hero and CTA loops six customer quotes with the Never Ending Story infinite snap-scroll pattern.

- `three@^0.160`, `@react-three/fiber`, `@react-three/drei`, `@react-three/postprocessing`

KEY CONSTRAINTS:

Avoid: ambient music, kinetic typography for its own sake, particles without semantic meaning, generic "scroll to explore" hints, generic "AI gradient mesh" backgrounds, em-dashes in copy.
- Token economics must be a hero metric. Show 331k â†’ 71k as on-canvas text inside the 3D scene, not as a marketing tile.
- LCP target under 1.8s on cable, under 3.5s on 4G. Total JS budget for hero under 280KB gzipped. GLB asset under 800KB. Mobile gets a 6-second MP4 fallback, not the canvas.
- No "Get a demo" CTA. Use "Start with one repo" or "Connect your stack" â€” Clean's product is self-serve.
- Never use the term "MCP" anywhere on this page.
- Accessibility: every scroll-locked animation must respect `prefers-reduced-motion: reduce` and degrade to a static stacked layout with no canvas.
